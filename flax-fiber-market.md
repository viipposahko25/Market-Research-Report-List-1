<p><strong>The Flax Fiber Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Long (bast) Fibers</li><li>Short (core) Fibers</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The flax fiber market is divided into three primary types: long (bast) fibers, short (core) fibers, and others. Long fibers, derived from the outer stem of the flax plant, are known for their strength and durability, making them ideal for textiles and industrial applications. Short fibers, sourced from the inner core, are softer and often used in composite materials or for insulation. The "others" category encompasses various by-products and niche applications, including biodegradable packaging and specialty textiles.</p></p>
