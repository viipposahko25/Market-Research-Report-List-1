Proton Exchange Membrane Fuel Cells (PEMFCs) are favored for their efficiency and rapid startup, making them ideal for light-duty vehicles. Alkaline Fuel Cells (AFCs) offer good performance at lower costs but are less commonly used in automotive applications. Solid Oxide Fuel Cells (SOFCs), known for high efficiency and versatility, suit heavy-duty vehicles and stationary power generation. Each type plays a crucial role in the growing hydrogen economy.</p></p>
