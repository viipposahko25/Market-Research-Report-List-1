<p><p>The multi-point locks market features two primary categories: Automatic Locking Multi-Point Locks and Regular Multi-Point Locks. Automatic locking systems engage multiple locking points simultaneously when the door is closed, enhancing security and convenience. These locks are often used in residential and commercial applications, providing superior protection against forced entry. In contrast, Regular Multi-Point Locks require manual operation for engagement, offering a balance of security and user control, commonly found in older door systems or specific building designs. Both types cater to varying security needs and preferences.</p></p>
