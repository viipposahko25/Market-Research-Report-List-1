<p><p>The rechargeable batteries market comprises various types catering to diverse applications. Nickel-Cadmium (Ni-Cd) batteries are known for their durability and ability to perform in extreme temperatures, though they suffer from memory effect. Nickel-Metal Hydride (Ni-MH) batteries offer higher capacity and are more environmentally friendly. Lithium-Ion (Li-Ion) batteries dominate due to their lightweight, high energy density, and widespread use in consumer electronics. Other types include lithium polymer and lead-acid batteries, each serving specific needs across industries like automotive, electronics, and renewable energy.</p></p>
