<p><p>The dyestuff for textile market encompasses various types of dyes, each suited for specific fabrics and applications. Disperse dyes are primarily used for synthetic fibers, providing vibrant colors. Reactive dyes react chemically with fibers for strong, long-lasting colors, mainly on cotton. Sulfur dyes offer dark shades and good wash fastness, while vat dyes, which are insoluble, provide excellent lightfastness on cotton. Acid dyes are designed for protein fibers like wool and silk, ensuring vivid hues. Other dyes may include specialty dyes for niche applications.</p></p>
