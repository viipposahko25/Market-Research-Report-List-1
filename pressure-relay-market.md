<p><p>The pressure relay market is segmented into four main types: plunger, diaphragm, spring tube, and bellows. Plunger relays utilize a mechanical plunger to detect pressure changes and trigger actions. Diaphragm relays employ a flexible diaphragm that responds to pressure variations. Spring tube relays utilize a coiled spring mechanism for pressure sensing. Bellows relays feature a flexible bellows that expands or contracts based on pressure, providing reliable actuation. Each type serves distinct industries and applications, ensuring precise pressure control and monitoring.</p></p>
